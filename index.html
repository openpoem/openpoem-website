Before the word

What exists before the words?

A word is a symbol — and the symbol is not the thing. Before the word apple is the apple itself. Meaning lives before the words: in rhythm, in how they are spoken, in feeling. OpenPoem goes there.

scroll

Every word is a symbol.

Every symbol points beyond itself.

Most people stop at the symbol.

OpenPoem asks a different question:

What exists before the words?

That is where ideas are born.

That is where meaning lives.

That is where we build from.

The meaning is already there

Open a word, and it remembers.

Pull a word apart and the meaning is waiting underneath the symbol. Tap one.

A making. To bring something into being that was not there before.

A word carries rhythm, intonation and feeling.

Written down, the symbol flattens all of it.

We are not machines reading tokens. Meaning is felt before it is named.

The instrument

Language is the only instrument that reaches it.

To get to what is before the words, you need the language itself — open, and unfiltered. We run open-weight models locally, on hardware we control: not the cloud's flattened, filtered version, but the raw representation, where the meaning still lives.

Local

The model runs on your own machine. Nothing is sent away to be shaped by someone else.

Unfiltered

Open weights, no safety-flattening in the way. The raw landscape of meaning, not a sanded version.

Yours

You own the model and what it finds. The search for meaning belongs to you, not to a provider.

Foundations

An old question, and a new one.

This is not mysticism. That the symbol is not the thing is one of the oldest questions in philosophy — and, suddenly, one of the sharpest in AI.

The word is not the thing

A sign is arbitrary; sense is not its reference. Saussure · Peirce · Frege · Korzybski ("the map is not the territory") · Wittgenstein.

Symbols alone do not mean

Manipulating form is not understanding it. Searle's Chinese Room · Harnad's symbol-grounding problem · Bender & Koller — form ≠ meaning.

Meaning is grounded and felt

It lives in the body, and in how a word is spoken. Lakoff & Johnson · Merleau-Ponty · affective prosody.

Before the token

A word is known by the company it keeps — and concepts live in a model's latent space before any word is chosen. Firth · distributional semantics · mechanistic interpretability.

Words remember

Meaning is layered into the history of a word, waiting to be re-opened. Owen Barfield, Poetic Diction.

OpenPoem takes a clear position in a live debate: meaning is more than form — and worth going after.
